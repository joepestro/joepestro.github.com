  • Browseology

    Co-founded Browseology with Art Chang. It's real time shopping with friends, no install required. Go javascript!

    Shopping is something you do with other people physically, but is tougher to do on the web. It's been largely a solo experience until now, but that instant validation can increase product conversion across the board.

    So give it a try, and let me know what you think. Copy & paste any Browseology URL to a friend and you'll be shopping together on the same page.

    http://www.browseology.com http://browseology.posterous.com

    Hope you like the sliding mechanism I wrote in jQuery, Kevin & Art's speedy implementation of oAuth, and the great design work by Rob Abbott. It was fun configuring our production server in about 30 minutes as time was running out.

  • Ping Later

    I really like iPhone development too. Objective-C is nice to me most of the time. Ping Later lets you schedule real phone calls from your iPhone.

    The interesting part here is that it's more than just the iPhone app. All scheduled "pings", as I call them, are sent to a server-side queue where they will be delivered on time regardless of your phone reception. Since the iPhone doesn't support apps running in the background, this works out to be the best solution.

    http://www.pinglater.com

  • Kiwis

    A simple URL shortener with a twist. You get a 10 second interstitial page to customize as you wish. Wrote this start to finish with Art Chang in about 12 hours.

    The hard part here was not the shortening itself. It was the overall experience of creating and viewing those shortened links. We kept it invite-only after launch, but recently opened up to everyone after we couldn't keep up with the number of account request notifications.

    http://kiw.is
