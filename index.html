  • Objective-C, Ruby on Rails

    Ping Later

    I really like iPhone development too. Objective-C is nice to me most of the time. Ping Later lets you schedule real phone calls from your iPhone.

    The interesting part here is that it's more than just the iPhone app. All scheduled "pings", as I call them, are sent to a server-side queue where they will be delivered on time regardless of your phone reception. Since the iPhone also doesn't support apps running in the background, this works out to be a nice solution that is reliable even if your phone is turned off.

    http://www.pinglater.com

    The Force

    This is a Firefox extension for binding TextMate to your Rails project. Decided that there must be an easier way to edit after looking for a view partial buried 4 levels deep. The Force provides a right click option to immediately start editing the controller, view, or partial you're viewing in Firefox. Open source, too!

    Has TextMate support now, more editors (and perhaps browsers) to come.

    http://github.com/joepestro/The-Force

    Kiwis

    A simple URL shortener with a twist. You get a 10 second interstitial page to customize as you wish. Wrote this start to finish with Art Chang in about 12 hours.

    The hard part here was not the shortening itself. It was the overall experience of creating and viewing those shortened links. We kept it invite-only after launch, but recently opened up to everyone after we couldn't keep up with the number of account request notifications.
